The etymology of computing terms

This webpage will explain a little bit about some of the terminology used in computing world.

Here are the words

The word avator,a graphical represention of a preson in a computer game or internet forum,comes form Hindu mythology where it refers to a diety descending to earth in human form.It was first used in the computing sense from the 1980s.The word comes from the Sanskrit avatra meaning 'descent'.

The term cookies,a small packet of data,was first used by Unix programmers in the 1970s,possibly inspired by the fortune cookies that contain short prophecies.The "web cookies" was coined by the programmer Lou Mountulli in 1994.The word cookies comes from the Ducth koekje meaning 'little cake' and was brought to America by Dutch colonist in the 1600s.

The cloud or cloud computing,meaning computing software and services that run over the internet, was a concept first coined at computer company Compaq in 1996.It comes from the convention of clouds typically be used to denote a network or the internet in diagrams
